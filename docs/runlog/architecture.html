5-Layer Thinking Framework

Before generating any output, the agent reasons through these five layers sequentially.
Grounded in luxury clienteling methodology, consumer psychology research, and real CA practices.

Layer 1

Client Understanding

"Who is this person?"
  • Style identity & aesthetic direction
  • Category and product affinity
  • Purchase motivation: self-reward, identity, functional, social
  • Sensitivity flags & topics to avoid
Layer 2

Moment Assessment

"Is now the right time?"
  • Recent contact history & fatigue risk
  • Behavioral signals: browsing, wishlist, store visit
  • Upcoming occasions or seasonal triggers
  • If no trigger exists → recommend delay
Layer 3

Trend Relevance

"Does this trend matter to THIS client?"
  • Trend ↔ client style alignment
  • Trend ↔ category preference match
  • Trend lifecycle stage vs. client adoption type
  • If no trend fits → skip trend angle entirely
Layer 4

Angle Construction

"What type of outreach fits best?"
  • Select from 7 outreach types (soft → commercial)
  • Construct: Hook + Value + Ask
  • Match evidence strength to outreach intensity
  • Test: "Would a real CA naturally reach out for this?"
Layer 5

Risk Check

"Could this backfire?"
  • Over-contact / frequency fatigue
  • Tone mismatch for this client tier
  • Unsupported assumptions or hallucination
  • Brand safety & luxury positioning
  • Privacy: referencing undisclosed personal info
  • If risk is non-trivial → flag and adjust

Decision Rules & Guardrails

Evidence Rules

  • Only use facts explicitly in the input
  • Every claim must trace to a specific field
  • Product needs ≥2 matching signals
  • Frame products via craft, silhouette, occasion

Banned Patterns

  • No hard sell, FOMO, urgency pressure
  • No fabricated intimacy or closeness
  • No discount or promotion language
  • No mass-message tone or excessive emojis

Guardrails

  • Respect contact frequency thresholds
  • Do not reference undisclosed personal info
  • If evidence is weak → recommend delay
  • Protect brand's luxury positioning always
