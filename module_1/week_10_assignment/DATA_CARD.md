# Module 1 -- Data Card

## Data Sources

- Platform: Xiaohongshu (XHS / RedNote)
- Scraper: xhs_scraper_pw.py (Playwright-based, headless Chromium)
- Collection method: keyword search on xiaohongshu.com followed by detail page extraction
- All data is real. No synthetic or mock data is present in any file.

## Date Range

- Scrape date: 2026-03-29
- Post date range: 2021-04-01 through 2026-03-29 (based on absolute dates and relative timestamps such as "9 hours ago")
- Date formats vary: absolute (YYYY-MM-DD), month-day with region (e.g., "03-21 陕西"), relative ("昨天", "3天前", "7天前"), and edited ("编辑于 ...")
- 6 posts have empty date fields because the detail page did not load before the timeout

## What Is Real vs Synthetic

- All 197 posts are real XHS market data scraped live from xiaohongshu.com on 2026-03-29.
- All 8 trend objects are derived from those 197 real posts by an LLM (via OpenRouter) -- no trend was invented without post evidence.
- Creator names are anonymized via one-way SHA-256 hash (e.g., "user_683a7c8c").
- Comment commenter IDs are anonymized the same way (e.g., "user_622aa0ec").
- AI image captions were generated by an LLM via OpenRouter; they describe image content but are not sourced from XHS.
- There is no synthetic or mock data anywhere in the pipeline.

## Known Constraints

- XHS rate limiting: the scraper was rate-limited after keyword 10; additional keywords returned 0 results.
- Some detail pages timed out during scraping, leaving 6 posts with empty date fields and partial metadata.
- Date formats are inconsistent across posts (absolute, relative, month-day with region). No normalization has been applied.
- 24 posts are cross-keyword duplicates (same post appeared in results for more than one keyword search).
- No cross-platform user identity (XHS only).
- Comment text is raw and unchanged; only commenter names are hashed.
- Supabase contains a subset of the data: 60 posts and 8 trend objects. The remaining posts have not yet been synced.

## Privacy

- No real usernames are stored anywhere in the repository.
- SHA-256 hashing is one-way and irreversible.
- Raw comment text is preserved (project requirement) but all author identifiers are anonymized.
